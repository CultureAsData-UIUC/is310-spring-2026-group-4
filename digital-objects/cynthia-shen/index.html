What this object represents

This page highlights menu examples that reflect how Americans structured meals during the 1940s, including breakfast, brunch, packed lunches (school and factory work), appetizers, and multiple styles of dinner.

These menu excerpts are derived from The Good Housekeeping Cook Book, a completely revised 1944 edition published by Farrar & Rinehart (New York). The menus illustrate common ingredients, convenience strategies (including canned foods), and typical meal sequences (starter → main → sides → dessert → beverage).

Selected Menu Excerpts

Breakfast (p. 161)

  • Orange juice; cereal; scrambled eggs with cheese; whole wheat toast; coffee; milk.
  • Grapefruit halves; bacon omelet; toasted English muffins; coffee; cocoa.
  • Strawberries and cream; poached eggs on toast; coffee; milk.

Lunch / School Lunch (p. 846–847)

  • Cream of tomato soup; crackers; carrot & cabbage sandwich; deviled eggs; molasses cookies; apple.
  • Vegetable chowder; peanut butter & marmalade sandwich; celery; packaged cookies; pear.

Dinner / Time-saving (p. 870–871)

  • Quick-seared hamburgers; buttered spinach; radish salad; bread; applesauce; sponge cake.
  • Canned onion soup; baked beans; tomato salad; canned brown bread; canned grapefruit; coffee.
